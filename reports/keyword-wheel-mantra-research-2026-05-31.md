## PART A — Keyword wheels / topical authority architecture

### A1. The three-tier hub-and-spoke ("keyword wheel") model

- **Primary (pillar / hub).** One broad, high-volume head term per topic. The pillar page gives comprehensive overview coverage and is the canonical destination for the head keyword (e.g. "Gayatri Mantra"). It does NOT try to rank for every long-tail itself; it routes authority outward and inward.
- **Secondary (cluster / spoke).** 6-15 mid-tail subtopics that each own one facet of the pillar (benefits, meaning, when to chant, deity-specific variants). Each spoke targets a distinct search intent and links *up* to the pillar with descriptive anchor text.
- **Tertiary (long-tail leaf).** Highly specific variants ("Gayatri mantra 108 times benefits", "Gayatri mantra in Tamil for students"). These can be sections within a spoke OR thin standalone pages — keep standalone only where real query demand exists (see B4 on bloat).

**Internal-linking shape that signals depth to Google:** the canonical hub-and-spoke wheel — pillar ⇄ every spoke (bidirectional), spokes ⇄ contextually-related sibling spokes (lateral, not exhaustive). Google reads a *tight, reciprocal cluster with clear hierarchy* as topical coverage; an arbitrary mesh reads as noise. This is the standard pillar-cluster model (HubSpot's topic-cluster framework; Semrush "topic clusters" guidance). Refs:
- https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/topic-clusters-seo
- https://www.semrush.com/blog/topic-clusters/
- https://ahrefs.com/blog/topic-clusters/

### A2. Tier structure for the mantra/stotra niche (worked example)

- **Primary pillar:** `Gayatri Mantra` (head term, overview + history + structure + how it generates the deity-Gayatri family).
- **Secondary cluster:**
  - Deity/planet Gayatris: *Hanuman Gayatri*, *Shiva Gayatri*, the 9 *Navagraha Gayatris* (see B2).
  - Intent facets: *Gayatri mantra meaning*, *…benefits*, *…when/how to chant*, *…rules (vidhi)*.
- **Tertiary long-tail:** *Gayatri mantra 108 times*, *…in Hindi / Tamil / Telugu*, *…for students/exams*, *…for peace*, *…meaning word-by-word*. Same template applies to every head mantra (Maha Mrityunjaya, Mahalakshmi, etc.).

### A3. Link counts, anchor patterns, page hierarchy (without looking spammy)

- **Counts (guideline, not a hard rule — Google sets no fixed cap):** pillar links out to *all* its spokes; each spoke carries ~3-8 contextual internal links (1 up to pillar + 2-7 lateral/related). Keep total in-content internal links roughly proportional to word count; avoid link dumps.
- **Anchor text:** mostly **descriptive/partial-match and natural-phrase** anchors ("Hanuman Gayatri mantra and its meaning"), with only *occasional* exact-match. Over-using identical exact-match anchors is the #1 spam signal — vary anchors and let context carry relevance.
- **Page-type hierarchy:** Pillar = long, comprehensive, evergreen (the entity page). Spokes = focused, one-intent. Tertiary = either a labelled section in a spoke or a leaf page only when demand justifies it.
- Refs: https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/links-crawlable · https://ahrefs.com/blog/internal-links-for-seo/

### A4. Interaction with AI Mode / query fan-out

Google AI Mode and AI Overviews use **query fan-out**: one user query is decomposed into many synthetic sub-queries, retrieved in parallel, then synthesized. A comprehensive pillar that *already answers the fan-out set* (definition + meaning + benefits + procedure + variants) is far likelier to be cited than a thin page answering one slice. Practical implications:
- Structure pillars with clear H2/H3 question-shaped headings, short answer-first passages, lists/tables — these are the **passage-level units** AI engines extract and cite.
- Each spoke supplies a citable answer to one fan-out sub-query; the cluster collectively dominates the fan-out for the topic.
- Refs: https://blog.google/products/search/ai-mode-search/ · https://www.semrush.com/blog/query-fan-out/ · https://searchengineland.com/google-ai-mode-query-fan-out-technique

## PART B — Mantra section taxonomy

### B1. Canonical members of each axis

- **Rashi (12 zodiac signs)** [confirmed classical — *Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra* (BPHS)]: Mesha, Vrishabha, Mithuna, Karka, Simha, Kanya, Tula, Vrishchika, Dhanu, Makara, Kumbha, Meena.
- **Lagna (12 ascendants)** [confirmed classical — BPHS]: the same 12 signs functioning as the rising sign. Lagna shares each Rashi's ruler, so its *remedial mantra* axis duplicates the Rashi axis (see B4 — this is a bloat trap).
- **Navagraha (9 planets)** [confirmed classical]: Surya, Chandra, Mangal (Kuja), Budha, Guru (Brihaspati), Shukra, Shani, Rahu, Ketu.
- **Weekdays (7) + presiding planet** [confirmed classical — vāra system, Sūrya Siddhānta order]: Sun→Sunday, Moon→Monday, Mangal→Tuesday, Budha→Wednesday, Guru→Thursday, Shukra→Friday, Shani→Saturday.
- **Festival** [modern convention for listing]: Diwali, Navaratri, Maha Shivaratri, Ganesh Chaturthi, Krishna Janmashtami, Ram Navami, Hanuman Jayanti, etc. (use the site's existing /festival set).
- **Deity** [variable by tradition]: use existing /deity members.
- **Reason/purpose** [modern convention]: wealth, health, protection, education, marriage, peace, obstacle-removal — use existing /purpose set.

### B2. Authentic classical mantra associations (sourced, no fabrication)

**Planet → Rashi rulership** [confirmed classical — BPHS Ch. on rāśi swāmi]:
Mesha & Vrishchika → Mangal · Vrishabha & Tula → Shukra · Mithuna & Kanya → Budha · Karka → Chandra · Simha → Surya · Dhanu & Meena → Guru · Makara & Kumbha → Shani. (Rahu/Ketu have no sign rulership in classical BPHS — a frequent modern error to flag.)

**Planet → bīja (seed) mantra** [confirmed classical — *Mantra Mahodadhi* of Mahidhara, 16th c.; widely reproduced]:
- Surya: *Om Hrāṁ Hrīṁ Hrauṁ Saḥ Sūryāya Namaḥ*
- Chandra: *Om Shrāṁ Shrīṁ Shrauṁ Saḥ Chandrāya Namaḥ*
- Mangal: *Om Krāṁ Krīṁ Krauṁ Saḥ Bhaumāya Namaḥ*
- Budha: *Om Brāṁ Brīṁ Brauṁ Saḥ Budhāya Namaḥ*
- Guru: *Om Grāṁ Grīṁ Grauṁ Saḥ Gurave Namaḥ*
- Shukra: *Om Drāṁ Drīṁ Drauṁ Saḥ Shukrāya Namaḥ*
- Shani: *Om Prāṁ Prīṁ Prauṁ Saḥ Shanaiścharāya Namaḥ*
- Rahu: *Om Bhrāṁ Bhrīṁ Bhrauṁ Saḥ Rāhave Namaḥ*
- Ketu: *Om Srāṁ Srīṁ Srauṁ Saḥ Ketave Namaḥ*

**Planet → Navagraha Gayatri** [confirmed classical structure — Gayatri "vidmahe…dhīmahi…prachodayāt" format; planetary set compiled in Tantric/Puranic sources and Mantra Mahodadhi tradition]. Note the epithets are **[variable by tradition]** (multiple valid readings exist across pañcāṅga/sampradāya), e.g. Surya: *Om Bhāskarāya Vidmahe Mahādyutikarāya Dhīmahi Tanno Āditya Prachodayāt*. Present these as "one widely-used form" and cite the variability rather than asserting a single canon.

**Weekday → deity** [variable by tradition — this is the most contested axis; state it explicitly]:
Sunday→Surya · Monday→Shiva (planet Chandra) · Tuesday→Hanuman (also Kartikeya/Durga regionally) · Wednesday→Vishnu/Ganesha · Thursday→Vishnu/Dattatreya/Brihaspati · Friday→Lakshmi/Durga (Goddess) · Saturday→Shani (also Hanuman). The planet↔weekday link is [confirmed classical]; the deity↔weekday link is [variable by tradition].

**Rashi → remedial mantra** is *derived*, not directly stated: BPHS gives the ruler; the bīja/Gayatri of that ruler is the remedy. So "Mesha remedy = Mangal mantra" is **[modern convention built on confirmed classical rulership]** — label it that way, never as a verbatim scriptural prescription.

Primary-source refs: BPHS (tr. R. Santhanam / G.C. Sharma editions); *Mantra Mahodadhi* (Mahidhara, tr. various, Prachya Prakashan); these are the texts to name on-page for E-E-A-T.

### B3. Recommended URL architecture for /mantra

```
/mantra                              (PILLAR: what mantras are, the axes, how to choose)
/mantra/planet                       (hub: 9 planets)
/mantra/planet/shani                 (spoke: Shani bija + Gayatri + when/how/source)
/mantra/rashi                        (hub: 12 signs)
/mantra/rashi/mesha                  (spoke: ruler Mangal → remedial mantra, "[modern convention]")
/mantra/day                          (hub: 7 weekdays)   ← cross-link, don't duplicate /day
/mantra/deity/hanuman                (spoke)             ← cross-link to /deity/hanuman
/mantra/purpose/wealth               (spoke)             ← cross-link to /purpose/*
```

**Cross-linking rules to avoid cannibalization:**
- `/mantra` axes own the *mantra text + classical association + japa vidhi*. `/stotra/*` owns *full hymn recitation*. Keep these intents distinct: a mantra page is short-form seed/Gayatri; a stotra page is the long composition. Link mantra→relevant stotra ("for the full hymn, see…") and stotra→relevant mantra, never replicate.
- `/mantra/day/*` should be a *thin wrapper that canonicalizes or links to* the richer `/day/*` and `/festival/*` pages rather than re-hosting content — or skip standalone day-mantra pages entirely and fold them into the planet spokes.
- Use `about`/`mentions` schema (Thing entities for each planet/deity) to disambiguate for AI engines, consistent with the site's existing schema strength.

### B4. Thin-content / index-bloat risk (6+ axes = combinatorial explosion)

Six axes multiplied = potentially hundreds of near-empty intersection pages (e.g. "Mesha + Tuesday + wealth mantra"). This is classic faceted-navigation index bloat. Safe approach:

**Indexable real pages (each has unique authentic content):**
- 9 planet pages, 12 Rashi pages, 7 weekday pages, the deity/purpose pages that map to existing rich sections, plus the /mantra pillar and axis hubs. That's a bounded, finite, defensible set (~40-60 pages) — each with a *distinct* classical mantra and source.

**Filters only (noindex or no URL):**
- **Lagna** — for the *mantra-remedy* purpose it's identical to Rashi (same ruler), so do NOT create 12 separate indexable Lagna mantra pages; make Lagna a filter/redirect to the Rashi/planet page, or a single explanatory page. **[This is the single biggest bloat trap here.]**
- Any **multi-axis intersection** (planet×day, rashi×purpose) → faceted filters with `noindex,follow` or canonical to the strongest single-axis page; never standalone indexable URLs.
- Festival×mantra → fold into existing `/festival/*`, don't fork.

**Controls:** `noindex,follow` on intersections, `rel=canonical` on near-duplicates, keep filter combos out of the sitemap, and only sitemap the bounded single-axis set. Refs:
- https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/crawling-managing-faceted-navigation
- https://ahrefs.com/blog/index-bloat/

**Bottom line:** index the ~40-60 single-axis pages (planets, rashis, weekdays, plus pillar/hubs), each carrying one verifiably-sourced classical mantra; treat Lagna and all cross-axis intersections as filters, not pages. This gives Google a tight, deep, non-spammy wheel and gives AI Mode a complete fan-out set to cite — without diluting the existing 930 stotra pages.
